Mono3DVLT: Monocular-Video-Based 3D Visual Language Tracking

Hongkai Wei, Qizhi Cai, Kimi Chat, Shijie Sun, Min Sun

Abstract

Harnessing both vision and language, Visual-Language Tracking (VLT) is emerging as a promising paradigm to bridge the human- machine performance gap in tracking. For single object tracking, VLT broadens the problem scope to text-driven video compre- hension. Yet, this direction is still confined to 2D spatial extents, currently lacking the ability to deal with 3D tracking in the confines of monocular videos. Unfortunately, advances in traditional 3D tracking mainly rely on expensive sensor inputs, e.g., point clouds, depth measurements, radar. Absence of language counterpart for the outputs of these mildly democratized sensors in the literature also hinders VLT expansion to 3D tracking. Addressing that, we make the first attempt towards extending VLT to 3D tracking based on monocular videos. We present a comprehensive framework, introducing (i) the Monocular- Video-based 3D Visual Language Tracking (Mono3DVLT) task, (ii) a large-scale dataset for the task, called Mono3DVLT-V2X, and (iii) a customized neural model for the task. Our dataset is carefully curated, leveraging a Large Langauge Model (LLM) and human verification, composing natural language descriptions for 79,158 video sequences aiming at single object tracking, providing 2D and 3D bounding box annotations. Our neural model, termed Mono3DVLT-MT, is the first targeted approach for the Mono3DVLT task. Comprising the pipeline of multi-modal feature extractor, visual-language encoder, tracking decoder and a tracking head, our model sets a strong baseline for the task on Mono3DVLT-V2X. Our extensive experiments include benchmarking by adapting existing methods to the proposed task and ablation studies.
